# **SaaS Analytics Project**

###### 1️⃣ **Data Sources**

The project utilized four CSV files as the primary data sources:

Users.csv → Contains user-level details, including signup dates

Events.csv → Tracks user interactions and activities

Payments.csv → Records transactions and subscription information

Season.csv → Provides seasonality and temporal context

The dataset simulates a real-world SaaS product and was used to build an analytics dashboard.

###### 2️⃣ **Tools \& Technologies**

SQL → Data cleaning, aggregations, and creation of intermediate tables

Microsoft Excel → Quick data inspection, cleaning, and filtering

Power BI Desktop → Development of an interactive dashboard including:

Revenue Trend Chart

Cohort LTV and Retention Matrix

Churn Percentage Table

KPI Cards

###### 3️⃣ **Problem Statement**

The objective was to monitor growth and retention for a SaaS product and extract actionable insights. Key metrics included:

Revenue trends and fluctuations

Churn percentages and areas of negative growth

Cohort-wise user retention and Lifetime Value (LTV)

Monthly Active Users (MAU) and engagement patterns

Key challenges identified:

Elevated churn in specific months (March)

Revenue decline following March

Inconsistent retention across cohorts

###### 4️⃣ **Insights \& Observations**

Revenue Trend: Smooth growth in February, a decline in March–April, followed by partial recovery in May

Churn Percentage: Highest in March; April ~50%, May ~100%

Cohort Retention: Strong diagonal retention indicates effective onboarding, but lateral retention remains low

Monthly Active Users: Approximately 2,000 users, consistent but exhibiting slow growth

Retention Rate: February 10.4%, March 8.3%, April 12.1%, May 9.9% → fluctuations indicate inconsistent engagement

###### 5️⃣ **Solutions \& Recommendations**

Challenge

Recommended Actions

High churn in March

Implement re-engagement campaigns, renewal reminders, and targeted discounts

Revenue decline

Focus on high-value cohorts and employ targeted upsell/cross-sell strategies

Retention inconsistency

Conduct cohort behavior analysis, improve onboarding, enhance UX

Slow MAU growth

Launch referral programs and targeted marketing campaigns

Engagement fluctuations

Collect user feedback, conduct surveys, and improve feature adoption

###### 6️⃣ **Conclusion**

Total Revenue: ~$3.98M

Monthly Active Users: ~2,000

Churn Rate: -1.07%

Retention Rate: 2.07%

Key takeaways:

Strong initial-month retention suggests effective onboarding

March churn and revenue decline require immediate attention

Cohort analysis provides strategic guidance for long-term retention

The dashboard effectively visualizes month-wise performance, user behavior, revenue trends, churn, and retention metrics
